C. VERY SHORT ANSWER (VSA) Type Questions

  1. What is arable land?
    (ans)
    Arable land is the land on which crops are grown.

  2. List the areas of nomadic herding.
    (ans)
    Arid regions of Sahara, Central Asia and some parts in India like Rajasthan, Jammu and Kashmir.

  3. Which countries are known for wheat productions.
    (ans)
    USA, Canada, Argentina, Russia, Ukraine, India and Australia.

  4. Which two countries lead in the production of jute?
    (ans)
    India and Bangladesh are the leading producers of jute.

  5. What is the main feature of plantation agriculture?
    (ans)
    In plantation agriculture, only a single crop is grown.

  6. Explain the two features of commercial farming.
    (ans)
    The two features of commercial farming are:
    1. The area cultivated and the amount of capital used is large.
    2. Most of the work is done by machines.

  7. What is mixed farming?
    (ans)
    In mixed farming, land is used for growing food and fodder crops and rearing livestock.

  8. What are the livestocks of nomadic herding?
    (ans)
    Yak, sheep, camel and goat.

  9. Name the major agricultural crops.
    (ans)
    The major agricultural crops are rice, wheat, millets, and maize.

  10. Which countries are known for rice production?
    (ans)
    China, India, Japan. Sri Lanka, Bangladesh and Egypt are known as rice production countries.

  11. How did the word agriculture originate?
    (ans)
    The word 'agriculture' is derived from two Latin words i.e. 'ager' meaning soil and "culture' meaning cultivation.

  12. What is pisciculture?
    (ans)
    Breeding of fish in specially constructed tanks and ponds is known as pisciculture.

  13. What are tertiary activities?
    (ans)
    Tertiary activities are those which provide support to primary and secondary activities.

  14. Why is agriculture a primary activity in India?
    (ans)
    Agriculture is a primary activity in India because two-third of India's population still depends on agriculture.

  15. Why are different crops grown in different regions?
    (ans)
    Different crops are grown in different regions because growing of crops depends upon the geographical conditions, demand of produce, labour and level of technology.

D. SHORT ANSWER (SA) Type Questions.

  1. What is meant by subsistence farming? Explain the types of subsistence farming.
    (ans)
    This type of farming is practised to meet the needs of the farmer's family. Low levels of technology and household labour are used to produce on small output. The types of subsistence farming are as follows:
    1. Intensive subsistence farming
    2. Primitive subsistence farming

  2. Define the following:
    1. Sericulture
    2. Viticulture
    3. Pisciculture
    4. Horticulture
    (ans)
    1. Sericulture : Commercial rearing of silk worms
    2. Viticulture : Cultivation of grapes
    3. Pisciculture : Breeding of fish in specially constructed tanks and ponds.
    4. Horticulture : Growing vegetables, flowers and fruits for commercial use.

  3. Give example of each of the following:
    1. Primary activities
    2. Secondary activities
    3. Tertiary activities
    (ans)
    1. Primary activities : Agriculture, fishing and gathering.
    2. Secondary activities : Manufacturing of steel, baking of bread and weaving of cloth.
    3. Tertiary activities : Transport, trade, banking, insurance and advertising.

  4. Describe intensive subsistence agriculture
    (ans)
    In intensive subsistence agriculture the farmer cultivates a small plot of land using simple tools and more labour. More than one type pf crop is usually grown. Rice is the main crop grown in intensive subsistence farming. Intensive subsistence agriculture is prevalent in the thickly populated areas of the monsoon regions of south, southeast and east Asia.

  5. Describe nomadic herding
    (ans)
    1. It is practised in the semi-arid and arid regions of Sahara, Central and parts of India
    2. In this type of farming, herdsmen move from place to place with their animals forfodder and water, along defined routes.
    3. Sheep, camel, yak and goats are most commonly reared

  6. Distinguish between Primary and Tertiary activities
    (ans)
    Primary activities are those activities which are associated with the extraction and production of natural resources. Tertiary activities are those activities which provide support to primary and secondary activities through services.

  7. Distinguish between subsistence and intensive farming
    (ans)
    Subsistence farming is practised to meet the needs of the farmer's family. It is done generally on a traditional basis using low levels of technology and household labour. A variety of crops are raised in small quantity on a small plot of land. Intensive farming It is a kind of subsistence farming only where a farmer cultivates a small plot of land using simple tools and more labour. This type of farming is common in the thickly populated areas of the monsoon regions of South, South-East and East Asia.

  8. What are the disadvantages of Shifting Agriculture?
    (ans)
    The disadvantages are as follows:
    1. Leads to large extent of deforestation.
    2. It leads to soil erosion and soil loss.
    3. It is only practised on small patches of land.

  9. What do you understand by agricultural practices?
    (ans)
    Agricultural practices are a collection of principles that are applied on farm production and the post-production process to produce safe and healthy food. Agricultural practices include certain steps: Soil preparation → seed selection → sowing → fertilizing → irrigation → crop protection → weeding → harvest and storage.

  10. Name the inputs, outputs and processes of agriculture in general. Also mention the various operations involved.
    (ans)
    1. The inputs in agriculture are seeds, fertiliser. machinery and labour etc.
    2. The processes involved in the agricultural operations are ploughing, sowing, irrigation, weeding and harvesting.
    3. The outputs from the system include crops, wool, dairy and poultry products.

  11. Explain the common features of commercial grain farming.
    (ans)
    1. In commercial grain farming, crops are grown for commercial purpose.
    2. Wheat and maize are common commercially grown grains.
    3. Major areas where commercial grain farming is practised are temperate grasslands of North America, Europe and Asia.

E. LONG ANSWER (LA) Type Questions.

  1. Write five points about Agriculture
    (ans)
    1. Agriculture is a primary activity as it includes growing crops, fruits, vegetables, flowers and rearing of livestock.
    2. 50% of persons engaged in agricultural activity in world and in India, two-thirds of population is still dependant on it.
    3. Agriculture or farming can be looked at as a system and the important inputs are seeds, fertilisers, machinery and labour.
    4. The operations involved are ploughing, sowing, irrigation, weeding and harvesting. The outputs from the system include crops, wool, dairy and poultry products.
    5. Agriculture is the backbone of Indian Economy

  2. Write five points about Shifting Cultivation
    (ans)
    1. It is practised in thickly forested areas.
    2. These are areas of heavy rainfall and quick regeneration of vegetation.
    3. A plot of land is cleared by felling the trees and burning them and then ashes are mixed with the soil.
    4. After the soil loses its fertility, the land is abandoned and the cultivator moves to a new plot.
    5. Shifting cultivation is also known as ‘slash and burn’ agriculture.

  3. List the types and subtypes of farming
    (ans)
    1. Subsistence farming
      1. Intensive subsistence
      2. Primitive subsistence
        • Shifting cultivation
        • Nomadic herding
    2. Commercial farming
      1. Commercial grain farming
      2. Mixed farming
      3. Plantations

  4. Write a few points about Plantations
    (ans)
    1. These are a type of commercial farming where single crop is grown.
    2. Require a large labour and capital. It is known as labour intensive.
    3. Usually it is processed on the farm itself to retain its freshness.
    4. Development of transportation network is very much essential for this type of farming.
    5. Example: tea, coffee, sugarcane, cashew, rubber, banana or cotton.

  5. What is Agricultural Development? How can it be achieved?
    (ans)
    Agricultural Development refers to efforts made to increase farm production in order to meet the growing demand of increasing population. This can be achieved in many ways such as
    1. Increasing the cropped area.
    2. Increasing the number of crops grown
    3. Improving irrigation facilities
    4. Use of fertilisers
    5. Use high yielding variety (HYV) of seeds.

  6. Differentiate between a farm in India and one in the USA
    (ans)
    A farm in India A farm in the USA
    1) India is a developing country that is agriculture-based. It provides employment to 60% of the total population of the country. 1) The USA is a developed nation that is not agriculture-based and only 2% of its entire population is employed in agriculture.
    2) In India the average farm holdings are small in size, i.e. 2 to 3 hectares. 2) In the United States, the farm holdings are of huge sizes, i.e. 250 hectares.
    3) Majority of the farmers in India are either uneducated or have very basic school knowledge from the village schools 3) The farmers in the USA are well educated and keep themselves up to date with the latest developments and technologies in the field.
    4) Indian farmers use man labour extensively with traditional methods and equipment like ploughs (still drawn by an ox). 4) In the United States, heavy machinery and advanced technologies are used in the fields to maximize their production.
    5) The farming in India is heavily dependent on the weather conditions and considering that the farmers only produce 2 to 3 crops in a year. 5) The farmers in the USA use scientific methods to study the soil, its contents, improve its quality, and use a variety of methods to irrigate their fields. This increases their productivity to multiple crops a year.
    6) Farmers in India get very little returns. 6) The US farmers get way bigger returns.
